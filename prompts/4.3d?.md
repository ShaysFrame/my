You're right that top-tier portfolios and landing pages have motion — but it's worth separating the concepts clearly:

## What "3D/motion" actually means in practice

There are three levels, and they're very different in complexity:

| Level                       | What it looks like                                                 | How it's done                                                                              | Effort                                    |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| **CSS motion**              | Smooth fades, slides, staggered reveals, hover effects             | Pure CSS keyframes / transitions                                                           | Low — you already have this available     |
| **Scroll-driven animation** | Elements animate as you scroll — text flies in, sections transform | CSS `animation-timeline: scroll()` or a small lib like **Motion** (formerly Framer Motion) | Medium                                    |
| **True 3D**                 | Rotating objects, particle fields, 3D scenes                       | **Three.js** or **React Three Fiber**                                                      | High — and often overkill for a portfolio |

The sites you're thinking of (AntiGravity-style, Awwwards winners, etc.) are almost always **level 2 with tasteful level 3 accents** — not full 3D scenes. Usually it's one hero element with depth/parallax, and the rest is really well-crafted scroll animation.

## The honest take for your situation

A pure black & white editorial portfolio **doesn't need Three.js**. What will actually make it look expensive:

1. **Staggered text reveal on load** — hero name/title letters animate in one by one
2. **Scroll-triggered section entrances** — project cards fade + slide up as you scroll to them  
3. **Smooth page transitions** — Astro has a built-in `<ViewTransitions />` component that handles this with zero extra dependencies
4. **One accent "wow" element** — a subtle noise/grain overlay, or a single CSS-only morphing shape in the hero background

That stack = pure CSS + Astro's `ViewTransitions` + optionally **Motion** for the React islands. No Three.js needed.
